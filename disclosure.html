How Affiliate Revenue Affects Our Recommendations

The short answer: it doesn’t. We research and write every guide before adding affiliate links. Our recommendations are based on which product is actually best for the reader’s situation, not which product pays us the most.

That said, transparency matters. Here’s what affiliate revenue does affect:

Which products we link to in roundups. When two equally-recommendable products exist and one has an affiliate program while the other doesn’t, we link to both, but the affiliated product gets the buy button. The non-affiliated product gets a plain text mention. We still recommend it; we just can’t earn a commission on it.

Which products we test. Affiliate revenue is what allows us to buy products for first-hand testing. A product we can’t generate any revenue around is less likely to get tested simply because the economics don’t work.

Our ability to keep publishing. Affiliate commissions are how we keep the lights on, pay for hosting, and (eventually) pay writers and editors to expand the site. Without affiliate revenue, this site wouldn’t exist.

What Affiliate Revenue Does NOT Affect

The recommendation itself. A higher-commission product never gets recommended over a better product for the reader.

Honesty about product weaknesses. We don’t hide flaws in products we earn commission on. Every guide includes honest tradeoffs and what each product is not good for.

Coverage decisions. We cover products based on what readers are actually shopping for, not based on which manufacturers pay best.

Your Choices

You’re under no obligation to use our affiliate links. If you’d prefer to buy a product we recommend somewhere else, please do — the recommendation still stands. The content on this site exists to help you make a better water-treatment purchase, period. If that means you buy somewhere else entirely, we’re glad the guide helped.
